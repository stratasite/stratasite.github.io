## Skip the Setup

Every other tool makes you commit before you can ask anything. Pick the right semantic view. Choose an explorer. 
Open the correct cube. Select a datasource. You have to know how the model is carved up before you even know what you want.

It feels like a non-issue the day you deploy a BI tool. Then the choices multiply into the thousands, and you're 
left asking which explorer had that flavor of revenue you were looking at last week.

Strata deletes the step entirely. Start with a rough idea, a measure or two you care about, and go straight into building. 
There's no construct to pick first, because the whole model is open to you at once.

That same property is what makes Strata safe for agents. Instead of being boxed into one explorer or cube, an agent can 
traverse the entire warehouse, and Strata keeps every step accurate as it goes.

## Feedback as You Build

As you assemble a view, Strata validates and previews on every keystroke. Add a measure and you instantly see the dimensions 
it can be sliced by. Add a dimension and incompatible measures disappear before you can pick a wrong combination. The answer
takes shape live, so exploration becomes a conversation instead of a configure, run, debug cycle.

No traditional BI tool works this way, and it's exactly why a Strata is loved by business users and agents.

## Guided to Valid Answers

The same interactive loop that guides a human, validate a partial query, surface the compatible next steps, reject 
invalid combinations with an explanation, is structurally an agent API. Whether the builder is a person typing or an agent
calling, every step stays grain-safe, and Strata steers toward answers that are both fast and correct. 

The default failure mode is a missed option, never a silently wrong number.

## How It Works

Strata uses a convention-over-configuration approach to semantic modeling, where naming does the heavy lifting. Give the 
same business concept the same registered name across your facts and Strata treats them as conformed and blendable. Give 
distinct concepts distinct names and they stay safely separate. That single discipline is the entire modeling protocol, and 
it's the simplest semantic model protocol there is.

From there, Strata reads your names, field types, and table relationships and compiles them into a refined hypergraph with 
proprietary extensions that powers extended relationship resolution across the warehouse. This is what lets a non-technical user 
surf broadly across the data: from a rough idea, Strata resolves which measures can be sliced by which dimensions and how facts 
connect, automatically, without an engineer authoring every queryable combination in advance. It was designed from day one for 
broad, accurate self-service over a messy warehouse.

This is the dynamic universe: instead of a curated model you must select before you can ask, the queryable space assembles itself 
around whatever you're trying to explore.
